Raices CTF OSINT Challenges

Question 1: Find the flag hidden in this image!

Note: The flag format for this event was flag{}


Step 1: Download the image.

After downloading the image, I inspected the photo to see if there was a visible indication of a flag being hidden in the image. Once I confirmed I was not able to see a flag, I decided to run strings on the image.

Step 2: I renamed the photo to osint1.jpg

Step 3: Open Terminal and run:

	    strings osint1.jpg | grep flag

Our flag is printed in the Terminal!

Question 2: What is the hotel this photo was taken from and what city?

Note: Time to see how good you are with your google reverse search!!


Step 1: Download the photo

Step 2: Go to Google, select the little camera icon, upload the photo we downloaded for this challenge!

Step 3: Banged my head against the wall repeatedly (Only kidding)

After going street to street, I finally stumbled upon a familiar view! We are close!!

Looking around, we see a hotel directly beside us! Just to be sure, I viewed photos of the hotel and was able to confirm, we have found our hotel!

The Hotel name and city is our flag!

Question 6: Keeber Security Group has a flag hidden. Can you find it?

After spending too much time going through their website and GitHub repository, I was stumped. I couldn't find anything relating to a flag. I had tried all the manual methods I could think of so I figured I would start with Terminal.

In Terminal, run:

	    whois keebersecuritygroup.com

We have finally found our flag!

When I think about finding the location of a MAC address. I think Wigle.

About Wigle:

Wigle.net is a website and mobile app that allows users to view and contribute to a database of wireless access points. The database is user-contributed and can be searched by location, network name, and other parameters.

The website also provides tools for analyzing wireless networks and mapping their coverage. The goal of the service is to provide a comprehensive and up-to-date map of wireless networks worldwide.


Now that we know the purpose of Wigle, lets dive in!

Step 1: Navigate to Wigle and create an account if you do not already have one!

Step 2: On the right side of the screen, you will see a BSSID field, input the MAC there!

Scroll until you find the pin of that MAC address!

Step 3: Zoom all the way in until you see the MAC belongs to a printer!

Click the device and you will be presented with all the information you need to answer these questions!
